## Reflection

### 1. What went well during this unit?

What went well during this unit was that our group was able to make the project idea feel a lot more real. The wireframes helped us picture how the main pages of the app will look and how users will move through the shopping experience. We also made good progress on the ERD because it helped us map out the main tables and relationships we will need for the database.

### 2. What were some challenges your group faced in this unit?

One challenge was deciding how detailed we wanted to be at this stage. We wanted the wireframes and ERD to be clear and useful, but we also did not want to overcomplicate the project too early. Another challenge was thinking through how parts like the cart, checkout, and orders should connect, since those features depend on both the frontend and backend working together.

### 3. What additional support will you need in upcoming units as you continue to work on your final project?

Going forward, we will need support with turning our plans into the actual app. That includes building the frontend pages, setting up the backend routes, and creating the database tables based on our ERD. _Pitch Draft (for our internal use):_

Hi everyone,

Our project is a full-stack e-commerce web app called Buylo.

Our app is designed to create a simple online shopping experience where shoppers can browse products, search for items, view product details, add items to a cart, and complete a purchase. On the other side, store managers can manage inventory by adding, editing, and removing products.

We chose this project because e-commerce is something people use all the time, so it felt practical, familiar, and realistic. It also gives us a good way to connect the frontend, backend, and database in one project.

Our main users are Shoppers and Store Managers. For shoppers, the main flow is browsing products, filtering or searching for items, adding them to the cart, reviewing the total, and checking out. For store managers, the main flow is managing the product catalog and keeping stock updated.

To plan the app, we created wireframes for the product catalog, product detail page, shopping cart, checkout page, and manager dashboard. We also created an ERD with tables like users, products, cart, cart_items, orders, and order_items. This helped us organize how the different parts of the app connect.

Our tech stack will be React, React Router, Node.js, Express, and PostgreSQL on Render.

Some of our main features will include product browsing, product search, category filtering, shopping cart functionality, checkout, order confirmation, inventory management, and form validation. Our two custom features we plan to focus on are category filtering and form validation.

Overall, our goal is to build something simple, useful, and realistic while meeting the course requirements and keeping the project manageable for our team.

Thank you.
